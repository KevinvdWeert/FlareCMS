Feature Article / Architecture

There is a specific kind of stillness found only at high altitudes, where the air thin and the world below seems like a distant memory. In the heart of Morocco’s High Atlas Mountains, a new architectural movement is taking root—one that doesn't just sit upon the landscape but emerges from it.

Fig 1.1 — The Obsidian Pavilion at Dusk. Photographed by Julien Roche.

Unlike the ornate riads of Marrakech, these structures embrace a brutalist purity. Raw concrete, local basalt, and floor-to-ceiling glass create a dialogue between the internal sanctuary and the external harshness of the peaks. Architects are no longer seeking to "decorate" space, but to frame the void.
